What we do during a claim-related inspection

We inspect the roof, photograph visible damage, note elevations and components involved, and outline a scope that is easier to compare with carrier paperwork. The goal is not to create noise — it is to make the condition of the roof easier to understand.

We do not act as a public adjuster. We are your roofing contractor. Once the carrier decision is in, we can help align the roofing scope with approved work and document supplements when additional roofing items are legitimately required.

  • ✓ Inspection photos — slopes, soft metals, flashings, vents, and supporting indicators.
  • ✓ Scope clarity — repair versus replacement path based on roof condition and damage pattern.
  • ✓ Supplement support — written roofing detail when approved scope misses legitimate field items.
  • ✓ Permanent work plan — scheduling the actual repair or reroof after the claim process moves forward.

FAQ — insurance claims

Do you meet with the adjuster?+

We can coordinate inspection timing and be available during site visits when helpful, but the key deliverable is still accurate roofing documentation and a clear scope.

Do I still pay my deductible?+

Your deductible is determined by your policy and carrier. We do not change policy terms; we help document the roofing work clearly and price it accurately.

What if additional roofing items show up after tear-off?+

That is where supplement documentation matters. If legitimate roofing conditions appear in the field, we document them clearly and submit the needed information for review.

Should I call even if the roof is not leaking yet?+

Yes. Many hail and wind claims start with exterior damage that is not yet causing interior water. Early inspection is often the smarter move.
