Membranes & assemblies

Flat roofs fail at transitions — parapet wraps, HVAC sleepers, and internal drains. We design tapered insulation when ponding is chronic, replace wet deck sections, and heat-weld or fully adhere seams per the manufacturer’s manual, not “what’s fastest today.”

Reflective white TPO can cut summer cooling load; EPDM remains a workhorse for ballasted or adhered builds. We’ll match the system to traffic, chemical exposure, and your warranty goals.

  • ✓ TPO & PVC — welded seams, reflective sheets, walk pad layouts for maintenance traffic.
  • ✓ EPDM — ballasted, adhered, or mechanically attached with seam tape or liquid flash where specified.
  • ✓ Modified & BUR — multi-ply capsheet work and flood coats at tie-ins.
  • ✓ Coatings & restorations — where substrate quality supports a manufacturer-approved recovery system.

FAQ — flat & low-slope

Why is water ponding on my flat roof? +−

Often structural sag, crushed insulation, or blocked drains. We map low spots with core samples and discuss re-leveling versus localized repair.

TPO or EPDM — which is better here? +−

Depends on reflectivity goals, foot traffic, chemical exposure, and attachment method. We quote apples-to-apples assemblies so you’re not comparing mismatched warranties.

Can you add insulation during replacement? +−

Usually yes — we model R-value targets for code and energy goals, and flag door or curb height impacts before you commit.

Do coatings replace a new membrane? +−

Sometimes as a qualified recovery over sound insulation and deck — not for saturated assemblies. We moisture-scan and pull cores before recommending coatings.

How long does a commercial flat re-roof take? +−

Square footage, tear-off depth, and weather drive the calendar. You’ll get a phased sequence before we mobilize so operations can plan around active areas.
